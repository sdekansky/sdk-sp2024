About this project

This project aims to define the development of Mexican national identity as socioeconomic divisions attempting to reconcile, set in the context of the Mexican Revolution of 1910. This era was the most formative in the construction of Mexico’s national identity because it saw this tension break out into a violent revolution that launched the entry of peasants and workers into local and regional politics, and therefore national imagination. We examine the art depicting colonial racial hierarchies in New Spain, the art of the Mexican Revolution, and contemporary Mexican art, in order to trace a general evolution of Mexican national identity. This is a multimedia project with two components: this interactive gallery website that displays artwork and analysis, and a research paper that goes into further depth.
